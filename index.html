HOW IT WAS DONE

Build the system first. Then generate the film shot.

The pipeline starts by constructing TRAPPIST-1 as a controlled Unreal scene. The AI stage is downstream from that scene, so it inherits the astronomy, camera move, timing, blocking, and scale.

01

Scientific source basis

The layout used public NASA and NASA-affiliated material, including the NASA Exoplanet Catalog, NASA Scientific Visualization Studio, NASA Exoplanet Archive, and NASA/JPL TRAPPIST visual development references.

The planets have not been visually imaged in surface detail. Measured data controls mass, radius, orbital period, orbital distance, star type, and received light; cinematic visualization fills in plausible surface, atmosphere, and lighting detail.

TRUE REFERENCE SCALE

Science layout

A reference map used one consistent conversion: 1 AU to roughly 11,644.44 meters in Unreal space. Under that scale, TRAPPIST-1e sits about 340.6 meters from the red dwarf.

CINEMATIC READABLE SCALE

Shot layout

A duplicate map compressed orbital distances so the system could be read in a single approach shot. TRAPPIST-1e was staged at roughly 65 meters and TRAPPIST-1h at roughly 137.5 meters from the star.

PRODUCTION RULE

Readable, not random

The readable version is a documentary staging layer derived from the science reference, not a new scientific claim or a generic sci-fi arrangement.

PROMPT TEMPLATE

The TRAPPIST video instruction block.

Use the uploaded Unreal playblast as the exact motion reference.
The playblast controls camera movement, camera path, timing, speed, lens perspective, object position, planet scale, orbital spacing, framing, and composition.

Do not change the camera move.
Do not change the planet positions.
Do not change the scale relationships.
Do not rearrange the system.
Do not add extra planets or nebulae.
Do not cut to a new shot.

Use the uploaded AI keyframe as the exact visual reference.
Match its lighting, color, red dwarf surface detail, planet texture quality, atmosphere, contrast, and high-end science documentary look.

This is the TRAPPIST-1 system: a compact red dwarf system with seven Earth-sized planets. Preserve the Unreal layout and scientific staging. The red dwarf is small, cool, and red-orange, not a Sun-like yellow star. The planets are compactly arranged, but their apparent positions and scale must follow the playblast.

Add only subtle physically plausible motion:
- gentle red dwarf plasma movement
- small corona shimmer
- slight atmospheric haze
- minimal planet rotation if it does not alter the composition
- very subtle star-field parallax

Final look:
photoreal cinematic science documentary, NASA/JPL inspired, BBC/National Geographic quality, realistic space lighting, high dynamic range, clean lensing, physically grounded, no cartoon or concept-art styling.

CLOSING NOTE

A controlled hybrid pipeline.

NASA data defines the system. Unreal Engine turns that data into scale-aware previs. The playblast locks the camera. AI keyframes define the target look. Seedance produces the final moving shot without being asked to invent the science from scratch.
